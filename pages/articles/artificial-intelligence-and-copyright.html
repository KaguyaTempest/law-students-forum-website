The Evolving Landscape of AI and Copyright

Examining copyright infringement in AI-generated images through the lens of cultural heritage and NFT creation

Author: Kudakwashe Kwitireni
Published: 10 June 2025
Reading Time: 8 min read

The rapid evolution of generative AI has ignited a fervent debate surrounding its intersection with intellectual property rights. Last month, I had the privilege of participating in a moot court competition hosted by the World Intellectual Property Organization (WIPO), where we delved into the complex issue of whether the utilization of generative AI, trained on datasets containing cultural heritage artifacts, to generate NFTs constitutes copyright infringement.

This essay serves as an analysis of this critical question, examining the potential infringement of copyright in AI-generated images and the broader implications for creators, consumers, and the legal framework governing intellectual property in the digital age.

Foundations: Copyright Law and Originality

Copyright law protects original works of authorship fixed in a tangible medium of expression. To be eligible for copyright protection, a work must exhibit a modicum of creativity and originality. This fundamental principle becomes complex when applied to AI-generated content.

Core Legal Principles

  • Originality Requirement: Works must originate from the author and possess minimal creativity
  • Fixation Requirement: Works must be fixed in a tangible medium
  • Authorship Question: Who qualifies as the "author" of AI-generated content?

AI-generated content, while often novel and innovative, may not meet the traditional standards of authorship. The question of creativity becomes particularly complex when algorithms generate content without direct human creative input.

The Human Intervention Factor

A key question in determining copyright ownership of AI-generated content is the extent of human intervention in the creative process. This analysis requires examining the spectrum of human involvement from initial prompting to final output curation.

Spectrum of Human Involvement

High Human Involvement

Detailed prompts, extensive curation, significant post-processing

Higher likelihood of copyright protection
Medium Human Involvement

Basic prompts, some selection from multiple outputs

Uncertain copyright status
Minimal Human Involvement

Simple prompts, automatic generation, no curation

Lower likelihood of copyright protection

If a human author provides substantial creative input, such as writing detailed prompts or curating training data, they may be considered the author of the work. However, if the AI model generates content autonomously, without significant human intervention, it may be difficult to identify a clear author under current legal frameworks.

AI Training Data and Copyright Infringement

One of the primary concerns regarding AI-generated content is the potential for copyright infringement during the training process. AI models are often trained on vast datasets that include copyrighted works, raising questions about unauthorized use.

Legal Precedent: Harper & Row Publishers, Inc. v. Nation Enterprises

This landmark case established principles for analyzing unauthorized use of copyrighted material. The court's analysis of the purpose and character of use, nature of the work, amount used, and market effect provides a framework for understanding AI training scenarios.

Fair Use Analysis in AI Context

The fair use doctrine may provide a defense against copyright infringement claims in certain cases involving AI-generated content. However, the application of fair use to AI is complex and highly fact-specific.

The Four Fair Use Factors Applied to AI

1
Purpose and Character of Use

Transformative Use: If AI-generated content significantly transforms the original copyrighted work, it may be more likely to qualify as fair use. This mirrors the transformative use concept established in Google LLC v. Oracle America, Inc.

2
Nature of the Copyrighted Work

The distinction between factual and creative works influences the fair use analysis. Creative works generally receive stronger protection.

3
Amount and Substantiality

The extent to which AI-generated content incorporates elements of the original work is crucial. Even small portions may be substantial if they constitute the "heart" of the work.

4
Market Effect

The potential impact of AI-generated content on the market for the original work is a critical consideration in fair use analysis.

Framework for Analysis

When evaluating AI use of cultural heritage artifacts, courts and policymakers should consider:

  1. Copyright Status: Is the artifact a creative work subject to copyright protection, or is it in the public domain?
  2. Degree of Transformation: Does the AI model simply mimic the style, or create something entirely new?
  3. Commercial Impact: Does the AI-generated content compete with or diminish the value of the original?
  4. Cultural Sensitivity: Are there additional considerations related to cultural heritage and community rights?

Platform Liability and Enforcement

Platforms that host or distribute AI-generated content face complex liability questions. The Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) provides a framework for addressing copyright infringement, but its application to AI-generated content raises novel issues.

Platform Liability Scenarios

Direct Infringement

Platforms may face direct liability if they actively participate in creating or distributing infringing AI-generated content.

Contributory Infringement

Following A&M Records v. Napster, platforms may be liable if they know or should know that their services facilitate infringement.

Vicarious Infringement

Platforms with the right and ability to control infringing activity may face vicarious liability, especially if they profit from such activity.

YouTube's Content ID system demonstrates how platforms attempt to balance automated detection with fair use considerations. However, applying similar systems to AI-generated content presents unique challenges in determining originality and infringement.

Future Legal Frameworks

As AI technology continues to evolve, legal systems worldwide must adapt to address the challenges and opportunities presented by AI-generated content. This requires both legislative action and judicial interpretation.

Navigating the Future

The intersection of AI, cultural heritage, and copyright represents one of the most challenging areas of contemporary intellectual property law. As technology continues to advance, legal frameworks must evolve to protect creators while fostering innovation.

Key Takeaways for Legal Practice

  • The fair use analysis remains crucial but requires careful application to AI contexts
  • Human involvement in the creative process may determine copyright eligibility
  • Platform liability frameworks need updating for AI-generated content
  • International cooperation is essential for effective regulation
  • Cultural heritage considerations add complexity to traditional copyright analysis

By understanding the legal principles and emerging case law that apply to AI-generated content, creators, consumers, and policymakers can navigate this new frontier while ensuring the protection of intellectual property rights and fostering continued innovation in the digital age.
