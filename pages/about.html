What is LSIF?

Law Students Intellectual Forum (LSIF) is the chill lawyer’s lounge for Zimbabwe’s legal community—an online commons where students from UZ, MSU, GZU, ZEGU and beyond trade ideas with practising lawyers, publish research, rate lecturers, share notes, and crowd‑source survival hacks for life in the Faculty of Law.

Think of it as a living study group, newsletter desk, moot‑court dojo, podcast studio, and career hub—all rolled into one responsive platform.

Our Mission & Vision

Mission: level the playing field for every Zimbabwean law student by putting crowd‑curated knowledge, mentorship, and opportunity a single click away.

Vision: a collaborative ecosystem where rigorous scholarship meets real‑world practice—shaping lawyers who are intellectually curious, ethically grounded, and socially conscious.

What You’ll Find Inside

  • Student Articles: peer‑reviewed think‑pieces and case notes.
  • Forum Newsletter: monthly digest of campus news & legal commentary.
  • Tutorials Guide: orientation hacks for lectures, libraries & deadlines.
  • Attachment Guide: turn unpaid internships into career launchpads.
  • Moot Court Hub: memorial templates, scoring rubrics, and oral tips.
  • Digital Library: textbooks, notes & past papers, continuously crowdsourced.
  • Rate‑My‑Lecturer: anonymous ratings + leaderboard (keep it civil!).
  • Legal Quiz Arena: weekly MCQ battles—XP and bragging rights included.
  • Locus Classicus Podcast: legendary cases retold by student voices.
  • Career Center: clerkships, internships, and CV polishing resources.
  • Community Forum (coming soon): open Q&A and networking threads.

Core Values

  • Collaboration: knowledge grows when shared.
  • Integrity: cite sources, respect copyright, speak responsibly.
  • Accessibility: free to join, free to contribute.
  • Innovation: blend tech & law to solve real problems.
