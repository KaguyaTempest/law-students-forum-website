Understanding Legal Attachment

Attachment is an integral part of the Bachelor of Laws program at the University of Zimbabwe. It provides invaluable practical experience, bridging the gap between academic learning and the realities of the legal profession. You will undertake attachment in two phases:

  • Second Year: Unpaid Internship - This introductory phase allows you to observe and assist in a legal setting, gaining foundational insights.
  • Third Year: Paid Internship - Building upon the first experience, this phase often involves more responsibility and provides financial compensation.

Navigating Your Attachment: Professional Conduct

Your behavior during attachment reflects not only on you but also on the University. Maintain the highest standards of professionalism:

  • Punctuality & Attendance: Be on time and maintain consistent attendance.
  • Respect & Demeanor: Treat all colleagues, clients, and court staff with respect. Dress professionally.
  • Initiative & Enthusiasm: Show willingness to learn and contribute. Take initiative when appropriate.
  • Confidentiality: Handle all client and firm information with the strictest confidentiality.
  • Seek Guidance: Don't hesitate to ask questions when unsure, but do your research first.
  • Feedback & Growth: Be receptive to feedback and use it as an opportunity to improve.
